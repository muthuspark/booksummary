Make Time by Jake Knapp and John Zeratsky

How to focus on what matters every day
Productivity And Habits
Author

Jake Knapp and John Zeratsky

“Make Time: How to Focus on What Matters Every Day” by Jake Knapp and John Zeratsky isn’t just another productivity book; it’s a practical guide to reclaiming your attention and focusing on what truly matters in your life. Instead of offering a laundry list of time-management techniques, the authors present a holistic approach built around two core principles: Highlighting and Scheduling. This framework, combined with a series of actionable strategies, aims to help readers actively design their days instead of passively reacting to them.

The book argues that our modern lives are dominated by a “highlight reel” of distractions – notifications, social media, endless emails, and a constant bombardment of information. This relentless stimulation leaves us feeling overwhelmed, stressed, and disconnected from what truly brings us joy and fulfillment. To counteract this, “Make Time” proposes a two-part system:

Part 1: Highlighting: This section focuses on consciously choosing what you want to focus on each day. It’s about intentionally deciding what activities will bring you the most value and satisfaction, rather than letting your day be dictated by external forces. The key strategies here include:

  • Choose a Highlight: The core of this approach is to select one or two important tasks – your “Highlights” – that you want to accomplish each day. These shouldn’t be trivial; they should be activities that align with your values and contribute meaningfully to your goals. The authors emphasize that these should be meaningful, not just urgent. Choosing your Highlight forces you to prioritize what truly matters, pushing aside less important tasks.

  • Identify your Highlight’s “enemy”: Once you’ve selected your Highlight, identify the biggest obstacles or distractions that are likely to prevent you from completing it. This might be social media, email, or even a particular person or environment. Recognizing these enemies allows you to proactively plan around them.

  • Timeboxing your Highlight: Allocate a specific block of time in your schedule dedicated solely to your Highlight. This prevents it from being squeezed out by other less important activities. The authors stress the importance of committing to this time and protecting it zealously.

  • Use the “Buffer” technique: Build a buffer zone around your Highlight time. This is a period before and after your Highlight to deal with potential disruptions or delays. This buffer allows for unexpected events without completely derailing your Highlight.

  • Be Mindful of your “Invisible Work”: The authors highlight the significant amount of time spent on tasks that don’t contribute directly to your goals, like endlessly checking email or scrolling through social media. By consciously recognizing and reducing this invisible work, you free up valuable time for your Highlights.

Part 2: Scheduling: This section builds upon the Highlighting process by structuring your day to maximize your focus and minimize distractions. Key strategies include:

  • Plan the night before: Rather than facing a chaotic morning, the authors advocate for planning your day the previous evening. This allows you to intentionally design your day and choose your Highlight before external forces can influence your decisions. This step involves setting up your environment, preparing materials and, most importantly, scheduling your Highlight time.

  • Use a visual schedule: A visual schedule, such as a printed calendar or a whiteboard, helps you stay organized and visualize your day. This makes it easier to see your Highlight and prioritize it accordingly.

  • Minimize interruptions: The authors suggest various techniques for minimizing distractions, such as turning off notifications, using website blockers, or creating a dedicated workspace free from interruptions. They even suggest techniques such as responding to emails only at specific times.

  • Schedule breaks: Contrary to the idea of constant productivity, the authors emphasize the importance of regular breaks. These breaks help to maintain focus and prevent burnout. They encourage deliberate, intentional breaks – not just mindlessly scrolling through social media.

  • Use the “Lazy Sunday” technique: Once a week (or at regular intervals), completely disconnect and disconnect from work and any planned activities. This reset allows you to re-energize and come back refreshed and focused.

Actionable Insights and Overall Philosophy:

Beyond the specific techniques, “Make Time” offers broader insights into our relationship with time and technology. The book emphasizes the importance of:

  • Intentionality: The entire system is built on the principle of conscious choice. Instead of reacting passively to your environment, you actively shape your day according to your values and goals.

  • Self-Awareness: The authors highlight the importance of understanding your own habits and patterns of distraction. This self-awareness is crucial for identifying your “enemies” and developing effective strategies to overcome them.

  • Flexibility and Adaptability: The system isn’t rigid; it’s designed to be flexible and adaptable to your individual needs and circumstances. The authors encourage adjustments and experimentation to find what works best for you.

  • Focus on the Present: The book subtly pushes back against the always-on culture by emphasizing the importance of savoring the present moment and disconnecting from constant digital stimulation. This isn’t about neglecting responsibilities but about being fully present in whatever task you’re undertaking.

In essence, “Make Time” provides a practical, adaptable, and empowering framework for reclaiming control over your time and attention. It’s not about working harder; it’s about working smarter and focusing your energy on the things that truly matter. By consciously highlighting your priorities and strategically scheduling your day, you can create a life that’s more fulfilling, less stressful, and ultimately, more intentional. The book’s enduring value lies in its straightforward approach and its empowering message: you have the power to design a day that aligns with your values and goals, and “Make Time” provides the tools to make that happen.
